# Navajo (Litogram)

Diné bizaad (Navajo) on a full US-QWERTY base; ł/ń/saltillo direct on AltGr, tone/nasal via two dead keys (acute, ogonek) that chain for the stacked nasal-plus-tone vowels.

Generated from the verified litogram layout definition
(`layouts/nv-US-design.json`) by keymapper's gen-os-artifacts —
the installable files teach exactly the arrangement
[litogram.org](https://litogram.org) does. Full design rationale:
the layout's page on litogram.org/keyboard-layouts/ and
CUSTOM_LG_LAYOUTS.md in the litogram repository.

## Sources

- Young & Morgan, The Navajo Language: A Grammar and Colloquial Dictionary (rev. 1987)
- The Unicode Standard (U+02BC saltillo, U+0142/0141 ł/Ł, ogonek vowels, U+0301 combining acute)
- Unicode UAX #15 (Normalization)
- US-QWERTY (base and shift layers)
